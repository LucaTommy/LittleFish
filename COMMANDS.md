# Part 2 — Features & Systems

## 🧠 Emotion System

Fish has **nine emotions** that shift in real time based on what's happening:

| Emotion | Baseline | Triggered by |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| **Happy** | 0.35 | Compliments, games, positive interactions, sunny weather |
| **Bored** | 0.10 | Long idle periods, no interaction |
| **Curious** | 0.25 | New apps opening, questions, interesting events |
| **Sleepy** | 0.00 | Late hours, rain, low energy, long idle |
| **Excited** | 0.05 | Games, snow, holidays, new high scores |
| **Worried** | 0.00 | Insults, thunder, CPU spikes, errors |
| **Focused** | 0.15 | Code editors, work apps open |
| **Frustrated** | 0.00 | Repeated annoyances, system issues, being shaken |
| **Content** | 0.20 | Calm periods, good weather, relationship milestones |

### How emotions behave

- **Momentum**: Emotions held above 0.4 for 60+ seconds become "sticky" — they decay 25–75% slower, making moods feel persistent.
- **Vulnerability windows**: After an emotion peaks and falls, an opposite emotion gets a brief boost (e.g. excited → sleepy wind-down, frustrated → content relief).
- **Frustration stacking**: Repeated annoyances within 5 minutes compound: 0.1 → 0.15 → 0.25 → 0.4 → 0.6.
- **Energy budget**: Fish starts each day at 1.0 energy. Activity costs energy (0.02 per behavior), idle time recharges it (0.03 per 15 min), sleep fully recharges.

### Weather effects on mood

| Weather | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Sunny/clear | happy +0.15 |
| Rain/drizzle | sleepy +0.1 |
| Snow | excited +0.15 |
| Thunder | worried +0.15 |
| Fog/mist | sleepy or curious +0.05 |

### Holiday boosts

| Date | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Dec 25 (Christmas) | happy +0.3 |
| Dec 24 (Christmas Eve) | excited +0.2 |
| Oct 31 (Halloween) | excited +0.3 |
| Dec 31 (New Year's Eve) | excited +0.3 |
| Jan 1 (New Year) | happy +0.3 |

## 🤝 Relationship System

Fish's relationship with you deepens over time, changing how it speaks and what it shares.

| Stage | Points | Typical timeline | How fish behaves |
|-------|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Stranger** | 0–49 | Days 1–3 | Formal, surface-level jokes, no personal sharing |
| **Acquaintance** | 50–149 | First week | Casual, shares opinions, remembers topics |
| **Friend** | 150–349 | Weeks 2–4 | Warm greetings, deeper humor, asks personal questions |
| **Close Friend** | 350–599 | Month 2+ | Uses your nickname, high vulnerability, deep unprompted speech |
| **Best Friend** | 600+ | Month 3+ | Maximum openness, complete comfort, personalized everything |

**Earning points:** Daily presence, active conversations, games, positive reactions, consistent returns.
**Losing points:** Insults, extended absences, shaking/abusing the fish.

As the relationship grows: openness rises (0.1 → 0.95), humor deepens (surface jokes → personal/contextual), the fish starts using your nickname, shows worry for you, and volunteers deeper thoughts.

## 🤖 Autonomous Behaviors

Fish acts on its own based on emotion, time, and context (~30-second ticks, 40% base trigger chance).

### Idle & ambient
- Fall asleep, wake up, yawn, stare off into space, stretch, look around, spin, sigh

### Time-triggered
- Morning greet (after 8 AM), lunch nudge (noon), end-of-day comment (5 PM), late-night concern (11 PM+), midnight sleep, Monday grump, Friday hype, hourly blink

### Curiosity
- Notices when YouTube, games, or other apps open; reacts to CPU spikes

### Attention-seeking (boredom escalation)
5-level escalation over 3-minute intervals when bored. Each level increases behavior frequency by 15%:
- Relocate → throw particles → random bounce → stare at cursor → little dance → blow bubbles

Resets when you interact.

## 🎮 Games

Three cursor-based desktop games where fish moves physically on your screen:

### Catch & Snack
Food falls from the top of the screen — move your mouse to guide fish below to catch it. Pizza (10 pts), donuts (15), apple (10), cookie (20), star (50 bonus), bug (−10 — dodge it!). Miss 3 in a row and fish gets visibly frustrated. Catch 5 in a streak for celebration particles.

### Flappy Swim
Side-scrolling obstacle course. Click/space to flap through green pipes. Gap sizes decrease as score climbs. Passing 10 pipes triggers a milestone celebration. Die immediately and fish says "I meant to do that." Die after a long run and fish is genuinely gutted.

### Whack-a-Bubble
Pop colorful bubbles before they timeout (3 seconds). Fish dashes toward clicked bubbles. 5 colors with different point values. Fast pops build combos. Miss 2+ in a row and fish covers its face in embarrassment. 45-second rounds.

All high scores saved to `%APPDATA%/LittleFish/high_scores.json`.

## 🧠 Learning Engine

Fish learns about you locally over time:

- **Learned facts** (up to 50): Preferences, habits, interests extracted from conversations
- **Behavior patterns** (100 sessions): When you're active, session durations, dominant emotions
- **Interaction quality**: What works vs. doesn't in replies
- **Response rules**: Generated every 50 interactions from quality data

All data stored locally in `%APPDATA%/LittleFish/learning/`. Raw data never leaves your machine — Groq is only used for distilling local data.

## 👁️ Screen Review

Say "look at my screen" and fish will:
1. Screenshot your primary monitor
2. Extract text via OCR (Tesseract, local)
3. Send extracted text to Groq for critique
4. Deliver 3–5 sentences of specific, actionable feedback

Five rotating review angles (senior dev, builder, UX reviewer, real user, tool builder) so consecutive reviews sound different. Optional focus modes: **design**, **code**, **copy**, **data**.

The raw screenshot never leaves your machine — only extracted text goes to Groq.

## 🐠 Fish Memory

Fish remembers things about you across sessions (up to 50 memories). Examples: "You're learning Rust", "Prefers dark mode", "Has a cat named Shadow".

## 🎤 Voice & Listening

### Input modes
- **Always Listening** — Continuous background VAD, triggers when speech detected
- **Push-to-Talk** — Hold hotkey (default Ctrl+Right) to record

### Speech recognition
Powered by Groq Whisper (API) with local fallback. Auto-detects English or Italian.

### What fish detects in your voice
| Signal | Response |
|--------|----------|
| Compliments | Relationship +5, happy boost |
| Insults | Relationship −2, worried |
| Name called | Immediate attention |
| Singing | Playful response |
| Loud spike | Startled reaction |

### TTS (text-to-speech) output
Three providers in priority order:
1. **ElevenLabs** (premium, cloud) — if API key configured
2. **Edge TTS** (free, natural) — default, always available
3. **pyttsx3** (offline fallback)

Auto-detects language for voice selection (English vs. Italian).
